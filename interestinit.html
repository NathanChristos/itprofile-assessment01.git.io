Nathan - Nathan’s interest in technology was awakened with the arrival of his families first computer. A roaring 8mb of ram and an integrated turbo button saw him exploring the wonders of MS Paint and Encarta Encyclopedia fascinated by the inner voodoo witchcraft taking place within this strange box. Many years later a friend would brag about his ‘computer building’ skills but for Nathan, the illusion was broken. Building is an art, a craft, builders create something from nothing. Prior to that day he had imagined computers being brought to life one capacitor, diode and resistor at a time in a mysterious dark lab just outside of reality. He wondered why the term building was used when computers essentially went together piece by piece like Lego. So he had to know more. Fast forward to today and he still craves a deeper understanding. A career in electronics had only fueled the desire to explore the fundamentals of computational processing and machine language so inevitably his journey found him enrolling with RMIT to study information technology. Nathans mission is to possess the knowledge and skills needed to develop any one of many inventive projects he has conceived.
